# Context

This reverse brief captures our understanding of the opportunity before moving into a formal scope or proposal.

It is designed to make sure the client context, priorities, constraints, and intended direction are aligned before any commercial recommendations are finalised.

# Understanding

- The client needs a clear, confident digital direction
- The experience should feel commercially sharp without becoming overproduced
- The work needs to clarify the audience journey and the decisions required to move forward
- The next stage should reduce ambiguity before detailed production begins

# Opportunity

There is an opportunity to translate early conversations into a focused strategic direction that can guide brand, content, design, and delivery decisions.

The reverse brief should make the shape of the project easier to see, easier to discuss, and easier to approve.

# Recommended direction

Start with a concise discovery and direction phase that turns the current ambition into a practical experience strategy.

The output should define the audience priorities, core message, key page or product moments, and the minimum scope required to create momentum.

# Scope notes

- This reverse brief is not a final proposal
- Pricing, deliverables, and production timelines can be confirmed after the direction is agreed
- Any assumptions should be checked with the client before moving into scope

# Next steps

1. Review this reverse brief
2. Confirm what feels accurate and what needs correction
3. Identify any missing context or constraints
4. Move into a focused scope or proposal
